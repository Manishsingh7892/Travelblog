RUSSIA


ST.PETERSBERG

St. Petersburg, Russia, founded by Peter the Great in 1703, is a city of immense cultural significance. Its architectural marvels, like the Winter Palace and the Hermitage Museum, house unparalleled art collections. The network of canals, often likened to Venice, adds to its charm, as do the enchanting White Nights of summer. It remains a cultural and historical gem.


MOSCOW

Moscow, Russia's capital, is a city of historical and contemporary significance. Its iconic Red Square hosts landmarks like the Kremlin and St. Basil's Cathedral. The city's rich cultural scene includes world-class theaters and museums, while its bustling streets and vibrant nightlife showcase modern Russian life. Moscow is a dynamic blend of past and present.


Arctic Circle (MURMANSK)

The Arctic Circle near Murmansk, Russia, is a realm of icy wonder. Murmansk, within this Arctic expanse, experiences the mesmerizing Northern Lights in winter. It serves as a gateway to the Arctic, offering icebreaker tours, reindeer sledding, and a glimpse into the rugged beauty and unique culture of this remote region. A true heaven for travellers


SOCHI

Sochi, a Russian city on the Black Sea coast, is a captivating blend of natural beauty and modernity. Known for its subtropical climate, it hosted the 2014 Winter Olympics. Sochi offers beautiful beaches, lush parks, and the stunning Caucasus Mountains. The city's vibrant culture and diverse attractions make it a dynamic year-round destination for tarvellers.


LAKE BAIKAL

Lake Baikal, in Siberia, Russia, is the world's deepest freshwater lake and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's renowned for its crystal-clear waters and unique biodiversity, including the rare Baikal seal. Surrounded by rugged mountains, it offers hiking, wildlife viewing, and ice-related activities in winter. Baikal is a natural wonder of unparalleled beauty.


SIBERIA

Siberia, a vast region in Russia, spans much of northern Asia. Known for its extreme climate, it features endless taiga forests, vast tundra expanses, and the mighty Siberian wilderness. The region is sparsely populated and is home to diverse cultures and unique wildlife, making it an enigmatic and remote destination for tourist from all around the world.


ALTAI

Altai, a remote Siberian region, is a natural wonderland known for its pristine landscapes. It boasts rugged mountains, deep valleys, and lush meadows, offering endless opportunities for trekking and outdoor adventures. Altai is also a repository of indigenous culture and traditions, adding to its allure as a captivating and off-the-beaten-path destination .


Kamchatka

Kamchatka Peninsula, in Russia's Far East, is a remote wilderness of volcanic wonders and untamed landscapes. It's home to over 200 volcanoes, geysers, and hot springs. The region's diverse wildlife, including brown bears and salmon, thrives in this pristine environment. Kamchatka's isolation and natural beauty make it a unique and thrilling destination.
